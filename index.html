The framework

The 7 Women's Empowerment Principles

Seven practical commitments — not a pledge, a checklist you can actually act on.

01

Establish high-level corporate leadership for gender equality

Make it a strategic priority set and owned at the top of the company, not delegated and forgotten.

02

Treat all women and men fairly at work

Respect and support human rights and nondiscrimination in hiring, pay, and promotion.

03

Ensure health, safety & well-being

Protect all women and men workers from harassment and unsafe working conditions.

04

Promote education & training for women

Invest in professional development that gives women a real path to leadership.

05

Empower women through the supply chain

Extend enterprise development, procurement, and marketing practices that support women-owned business.

06

Champion equality through community initiatives

Use the company's voice and reach to advocate for equality beyond its own walls.

07

Measure & publicly report progress

Track gender-disaggregated data and publish it — accountability is what makes this more than a statement.

What signing actually means

The questions every CEO asks before signing

Who actually has to sign?

The CEO or highest-ranking equivalent at the company — not HR, not a delegate. It's a personal statement of commitment from the top, for companies with a minimum of 10 employees.

Does it cost anything?

No. Joining WEPs is free. There's no membership fee, no minimum spend, no consultancy requirement.

Is our signature made public?

Your company's join date becomes public on the WEPs signatory list. The signed statement document itself is kept internal to UN Women, not published.

What are we actually committing to?

Using the 7 Principles as a guide for internal action, and reporting progress over time using gender-disaggregated data — you set your own pace and targets.

What happens right after signing?

You get access to WEPs' tools — including the Gender Gap Analysis Tool and reporting guidance — to help translate the commitment into an actual internal plan.
