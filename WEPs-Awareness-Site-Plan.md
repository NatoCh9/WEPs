# WEPs Awareness Page — Site Plan

## 1. Goal & Positioning

A single-page awareness site whose sole conversion goal is to move a business decision-maker from
*"never heard of WEPs"* → *"I want my CEO to sign the Statement of Support."*

This is **not** a replacement for [weps.org](https://www.weps.org/) — it's a feeder/awareness page.
Every primary CTA links out to the official WEPs join flow (`weps.org/join` and the
[CEO Statement of Support](https://www.weps.org/resource/ceo-statement-support)). We are not collecting
signatures ourselves, we're generating warm traffic to the official process.

**Origin story we tell:** WEPs was established by UN Women (in partnership with UN Global Compact) as
the leading framework for corporate gender-equality practice. We borrow that credibility explicitly —
this page positions itself as an independent advocacy/explainer resource, not an official UN Women
channel, so language should say "learn about" / "here's why to join," never imply we *are* UN Women.

## 2. Audience

General business audience — works whether the visitor runs a 20-person company or a multinational.
Framing stays business-case-first (competitive advantage, talent, risk/ESG) with ethics as reinforcement,
not the lead — this is the tone that both WEPs and Catalyst use successfully, and it reads credibly to
skeptical decision-makers who aren't already converted.

No regional or sector narrowing — copy and examples stay global/generic.

## 3. Site Structure (single scrolling page)

| # | Section | Purpose |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | Hero | Hook + immediate framing: what WEPs is, in one sentence, + primary CTA |
| 2 | Trust bar | Borrowed credibility: "Established by UN Women & UN Global Compact" + signatory count stat |
| 3 | The Business Case | Why gender equality at work is a performance issue, not just an ethics one |
| 4 | The 7 Principles | Scannable numbered list, one line each, no walls of text |
| 5 | **Quiz: "How WEPs-Aware Are You?"** | Fun knowledge test that converts curiosity into the CEO-invite ask; doubles as a standalone shareable asset for social ads |
| 6 | Proof it works | 2–3 short signatory examples/quotes (real, sourced from WEPs materials — not fabricated) |
| 7 | What signing actually means | Demystify the ask: who signs (CEO), what's required, what it costs (nothing), what happens after |
| 8 | Resources | Links out to official WEPs tools (Gender Gap Analysis Tool, Transparency & Accountability Roadmap) |
| 9 | **Newsletter signup** | Low-friction email capture to keep non-converters warm ("hear updates") |
| 10 | Final CTA | Repeat the ask, lower the friction, link to official join page |
| 11 | Footer | Attribution to UN Women/UN Global Compact as the WEPs originators, disclaimer that this is an independent awareness resource, link to official site |

## 4. Content Direction Per Section

**Hero**
- Headline blends the two poles the 8-site teardown identifies (emotional/movement vs. business-case) —
  this is the "deliberate blender" approach the teardown flags as best-optimized for corporate audiences
  (Catalyst, Lean In): one aspirational line + one hard proof-point stat, not either alone
  - Headline candidate: *"Equality means business — literally."* (nods to WEPs' own tagline, keeps it punchy)
  - Stat sub-line directly under/beside it: *"12,000+ CEOs in 160 countries have already signed. Has yours?"*
- Subhead: one sentence explaining WEPs = UN Women's global framework for gender equality in business
- Two CTAs, verb friction matched to visitor temperature (per teardown's CTA-staging pattern — soft verbs
  for cold/first-time traffic, hard verbs only once they're warm):
  - Primary (soft, cold traffic): **"See the 7 Principles"** (scrolls down) — an explore/learn verb, not an ask
  - Secondary (hard, for already-convinced visitors): **"Sign the CEO Statement"** (external link to weps.org)
- Single focused hero (not a rotating carousel) — Cherie Blair and Lean In show a clean single-message
  hero converts fine for this kind of page, and a carousel adds build complexity with no real payoff here

**Trust bar**
- Small logo/text strip: "An initiative of UN Women & UN Global Compact" + stat: "12,000+ CEOs signed
  globally, across 160 countries"
- Add a **signatory logo wall** directly beneath — the teardown shows this "trusted by" pattern (Lean In:
  Walmart/P&G/NBA/MetLife/Mattel logos) is doing real credibility work independent of the stat callout;
  our version pulls real logos from WEPs' public signatory list, not fabricated ones
- Keeps this page from reading as a random third-party site — borrows the social-proof pattern common
  across nearly every researched site

**The Business Case**
- 4 benefit cards, directly modeled on Catalyst's "Inclusion is a business imperative" card band (the
  teardown's clearest example of translating cause into ROI): **Attract & retain talent** · **Drive
  innovation & performance** · **Build trust & reputation** · **Meet ESG & investor expectations**
- Each card = one bold 3–5 word header + one supporting sentence, no paragraphs — mirrors Catalyst's card
  format exactly since the teardown flags it as the most-copied structure for this exact audience

**The 7 Principles**
- Numbered 1–7, each principle = bolded 4–6 word title + one-sentence plain-language explanation
  (pull faithfully from the official WEPs/UN Global Compact wording, don't rephrase into vagueness)
- No jargon-only bullets — this is the section most visitors will actually read, so it needs to be the
  clearest thing on the page

**Quiz: "How WEPs-Aware Are You?"**
- 6–8 multiple-choice questions mixing two angles: (a) WEPs facts (what it is, who created it, how many
  companies signed, what signing costs) and (b) workplace-protection knowledge the Principles actually
  cover (equal pay, harassment/safety, parental leave, health, leadership representation) — the second
  angle is what makes it feel personally relevant instead of trivia
- Immediate one-line feedback after each answer ("Correct — WEPs covers this under Principle 3: health,
  safety & freedom from violence") so the quiz teaches as you go, not just tests
- Score-gated results, 3 tiers, each ending in the same core ask but tuned in tone:
  - Low score → "You're not alone — most people haven't heard of WEPs either. Here's the 60-second fix:
    forward this to your CEO."
  - Mid score → "You know more than most. Time to make it official — invite your CEO to sign."
  - High score → "WEPs Champion. You clearly get it — now put your company where your knowledge is."
  All three tiers end with the same two buttons: **Invite your CEO** (mailto/share template) and
  **Share your score** (social share)
- Built as a self-contained, mobile-first module so it can be lifted out and run as a standalone landing
  page for paid social ads (short version, 5 questions, same result-tier logic) — the on-site version can
  be the longer 8-question cut
- Keep it light in tone (quiz-show energy) while questions themselves stay accurate — this is the
  section most likely to get shared, so factual errors here are the most reputationally costly on the page

**Proof it works**
- 2–3 pull-quotes or short case mentions from real signatory companies (sourced from official WEPs
  "field stories" / progress reports — flag placeholders clearly if we can't verify a real one before build)

**What signing actually means**
- Plain FAQ-style micro-copy: Who signs? (CEO/equivalent, 10+ employees minimum) · Is it public? (join
  date is public, the statement itself is kept internal to UN Women) · Does it cost anything? (no) ·
  What's the commitment? (report progress via gender-disaggregated data over time)
- This directly addresses the biggest conversion blocker: decision-makers not signing because they don't
  understand the ask

**Resources**
- Cards linking to official WEPs tools: Gender Gap Analysis Tool, Transparency & Accountability Roadmap,
  Progress Reports — positions this page as a helpful hub, not a dead end

**Newsletter signup**
- Single email field, one-line value prop ("Get one email when new companies sign, new tools drop, or
  we publish a new quiz") — no multi-field forms, friction kills sign-up rate here
- Positioned as the soft-conversion option for visitors who took the quiz/read the page but aren't ready
  to push their CEO yet — keeps them reachable instead of losing them entirely
- Needs a real email service to actually collect addresses (Mailchimp/ConvertKit/Buttondown embed form,
  or similar) — a static HTML page can't store submissions on its own, see open question below

**Final CTA**
- Restate the ask plainly: "Invite your CEO to sign" + button to official CEO Statement of Support page

**Footer**
- Clear attribution: "Women's Empowerment Principles were created by UN Women in partnership with the UN
  Global Compact. This page is an independent awareness resource and is not an official UN Women site."
  Link to weps.org.

## 5. CTA Verb Discipline (cross-cutting rule)

Per the 8-site teardown's clearest cross-cutting finding: soft discovery verbs for cold traffic, hard
commitment verbs only once a visitor is warmed up. Applied across this page:

| Section | Verb temperature | Verb |
|---|---|---|
| Hero primary | Cold | "See the 7 Principles" |
| 7 Principles → Quiz | Cold/curious | "Test your knowledge" |
| Quiz results | Warm (just engaged) | "Invite your CEO" / "Share your score" |
| Resources | Cold | "Explore the toolkit" |
| Newsletter | Cold, low-commitment | "Get updates" (not "Subscribe" — softer) |
| Final CTA | Warm (reached end of page) | "Sign the CEO Statement" |

Rule of thumb: never put a hard-ask verb ("Sign," "Donate," "Commit") above the fold or before the
visitor has had at least one substantive section of content — this is what separates WEPs/Catalyst
(stage the ask) from a page that asks too early and gets ignored.

## 7. Visual Direction

Following the pattern the researched sites (including the 8-site teardown) converge on:
- Professional, restrained palette — not stereotypically "pink/feminine," reads corporate-trustworthy
  (this matters for credibility with the audience we're targeting)
- Bold, confident typography for stats and section headers
- Diverse workplace photography (if/when we source images) rather than iconography-only
- Generous whitespace, one clear CTA color used consistently so it stands out every time it appears
- Mobile-first — most of this traffic (social/LinkedIn referral) will land on mobile

## 8. Open Questions Before Build

1. **Real quotes/case studies** — do we have permission or sourcing to pull specific company quotes from
   WEPs materials, or should section 6 launch with generic/placeholder examples clearly marked as such?
2. **Hosting/tech** — confirmed as an HTML/CSS(/JS) static page for now. Any preference on plain
   HTML+CSS vs. a lightweight framework, or is a single self-contained HTML file fine?
3. **Branding** — any existing logo, color, or name for this awareness initiative, or should one be
   proposed as part of the build?
4. **Newsletter backend** — a static page has nowhere to send submitted emails. Do you already have (or
   want me to set up) a form endpoint — Mailchimp/ConvertKit/Buttondown/Formspree-style embed — or should
   the newsletter form ship as UI-only (non-functional) until that's decided?
5. **Quiz question sourcing** — I'll draft quiz questions from the same official WEPs/UN Global Compact
   material used elsewhere on the page (7 Principles text, CEO Statement of Support facts) so nothing is
   invented. Fine to proceed on that basis, or do you have specific facts/stats you want included?
6. **Standalone ad version of the quiz** — build the shorter 5-question ad-ready version now alongside
   the main page, or ship the on-site 8-question version first and split out the ad variant later?
